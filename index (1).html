The Problem

Harvest now, decrypt later isn't theoretical.

Adversaries can record encrypted traffic today and hold it until a cryptographically relevant quantum computer can break it. For data that needs to stay confidential for years, that machine doesn't need to exist yet — it just needs to exist before your secrets expire.

Shor's algorithm breaks the math behind RSA, Diffie-Hellman, and elliptic-curve cryptography once a large enough fault-tolerant quantum computer exists. Most estimates put that milestone somewhere in the next decade — nobody can say the exact date, which is exactly the problem.

Sentinel doesn't try to predict Q-Day. It tracks your exposure: which systems still rely on algorithms with an expiration date, and how far along your migration to post-quantum cryptography actually is.

How It Works

Four stages, running continuously.

Sentinel isn't a one-time audit. Each stage re-runs on a schedule, so readiness is a number you can watch move, not a report that goes stale.

01Discover

Scan the estate

Crawl certificates, TLS configs, code dependencies, and key stores across cloud and on-prem systems.

02Assess

Score exposure

Weigh each finding by data sensitivity, retention window, and how exploitable it is once quantum-capable hardware exists.

03Migrate

Track remediation

Assign owners, link tickets, and follow each system through its move to a post-quantum algorithm.

04Verify

Confirm and re-scan

Re-check migrated systems on a cadence to catch regressions before they become incidents.
